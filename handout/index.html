Skills

/aaa When "looks good to me" isn't enough. Stress-tests an idea, feature, or design against real-world best practices and hands you 3–4 concrete ways to make it world-class. /documentation-standard Docs your team will actually find again. Creates and refactors architecture notes, ADRs, manuals, and dev guides with enforced structure — nothing gets lost in a folder. /llm-wiki You've done the research — but Claude keeps forgetting it. Captures your notes, sources, and decisions in one place; future chats search it first → sharper answers, fewer tokens. /llm-wiki-product Know exactly where you lose to competitors. Add rivals to your project wiki; Claude finds the gaps in your product and hands back a prioritized backlog ranked by value vs. effort. /plan-maker Stop staring at a ticket wondering where to start. Breaks a feature into the smallest tasks with tests and dependencies — ready for /implement-next to chew through. /skill-packager Built a Claude Code skill? Make it work in Claude Desktop too. Packages your skill folder into an upload-ready ZIP for Desktop and claude.ai — names, paths, and frontmatter handled.

Commands

/da-review A second opinion that actually pushes back. Runs a one-pass devil's-advocate review of your code, plan, or design and lists every flaw it can find — no auto-fixes, just honest verdicts. /feature-refinement Turn a rough idea into a brief you can hand off. A senior product thinker walks you through the questions you'd otherwise skip, and lands you with a scoped Feature Brief ready for /plan-maker. /implement-all Have a finished plan? Walk away and let it ship. Runs /implement-next in a loop — one task, one commit at a time — until every item in the plan is built, reviewed, and checked off. Works in any harness. /implement-next Stop juggling the whole plan in your head. Picks the next unchecked task in a plan file, builds it test-first, runs parallel reviews, fixes what they flag, then commits — one task, done. Works in any harness. /iterative-review A review that doesn't stop at finding problems. Multiple devil's-advocate agents stress-test your work in parallel; fix agents patch every major issue; loops until the pass is clean.

Agents

devils-advocate The agent that hunts for what can break. Stress-tests a plan, code, or recommendation by attacking assumptions and exposing blind spots — used inside /da-review and /iterative-review.

Scripts

scripts-logging Never lose a Claude conversation again. Every prompt you submit is quietly saved into per-project Markdown logs, organized by session — searchable, shareable, yours to keep. scripts-plan Keep plan execution honest, one commit at a time. Tracks progress through a plan file, enforces one-commit-per-task discipline, and audits a finished run end-to-end.
