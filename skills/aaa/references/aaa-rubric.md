# AAA Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric as a judgment aid, not as a rigid checklist. Select the dimensions relevant to the user's request.

## Rating scale

- 1: Weak or risky
- 2: Below professional quality
- 3: Acceptable / professional baseline
- 4: Strong
- 5: AAA / world-class candidate

Do not average scores mechanically. A single severe weakness can prevent AAA status.

## General idea dimensions

### 1. Problem intensity

Ask:

- Is the problem painful, frequent, expensive, risky, or emotionally important?
- Is the user already trying to solve it with workarounds?
- Would the target user care enough to switch behavior?

AAA signal: the problem is specific, urgent, and clearly worth solving.

### 2. User value

Ask:

- What user outcome improves?
- Is the benefit measurable?
- Is the value obvious within seconds?

AAA signal: the idea creates a large improvement in time, money, confidence, quality, safety, clarity, or enjoyment.

### 3. Differentiation

Ask:

- Why this instead of existing alternatives?
- Is the difference structural or merely cosmetic?
- Is there a defensible insight?

AAA signal: the concept has a clear wedge, not just feature parity.

### 4. Simplicity and elegance

Ask:

- Can the idea be explained in one sentence?
- Is complexity hidden from the user?
- Are there unnecessary features or abstractions?

AAA signal: the experience feels obvious after seeing it.

### 5. Feasibility

Ask:

- Can this be built with available technology, budget, talent, and time?
- What assumptions must be true?
- What is the hardest part?

AAA signal: the path is ambitious but technically and operationally credible.

### 6. Execution quality

Ask:

- What would make this feel polished?
- Where can the product fail under real usage?
- What details will users notice immediately?

AAA signal: edge cases, onboarding, defaults, empty states, speed, language, and recovery paths are designed intentionally.

### 7. Strategic leverage

Ask:

- Does the idea create compounding advantage?
- Does usage make the product better?
- Does it generate data, workflow lock-in, brand trust, or ecosystem leverage ethically?

AAA signal: the product gets stronger as it is used.

### 8. Scalability and durability

Ask:

- Can the idea scale technically, economically, and organizationally?
- Will quality degrade as usage grows?
- Is the maintenance burden justified?

AAA signal: scale improves economics or usefulness without destroying quality.

### 9. Risk and trust

Ask:

- What could harm users, reputation, compliance, security, privacy, or reliability?
- What could be misunderstood?
- What must be explainable or reversible?

AAA signal: trust is designed into the system, not patched on later.

### 10. Evidence alignment

Ask:

- What do research, competitors, standards, or field evidence suggest?
- Does the idea contradict proven practice?
- Is there a credible reason to deviate from convention?

AAA signal: the concept is informed by evidence and improves on it deliberately.

## Interpretation

- 5-level quality requires strength in the most important dimensions and no fatal weakness.
- A narrow but excellent idea can be more AAA than a broad unfocused one.
- An idea can be technically impressive and still not be AAA if the user value is weak.
- An idea can be simple and still AAA if the insight is strong and execution quality is high.
