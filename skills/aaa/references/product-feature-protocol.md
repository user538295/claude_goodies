# Product and Feature Protocol

Use this for product ideas, feature ideas, UX concepts, AI features, apps, services, business models, and go-to-market concepts.

## Product review sequence

1. Define the target user and job-to-be-done.
2. Identify the painful moment the idea addresses.
3. Identify existing alternatives and why users tolerate them.
4. Determine the user's proposed advantage.
5. Research best-in-class products and current practices.
6. Evaluate whether the idea can win on usefulness, simplicity, trust, speed, cost, or delight.
7. Propose 3-4 upgrade paths.

## Product questions

### User and problem

- Who exactly is this for?
- What situation triggers usage?
- What is the current workaround?
- What would make the user say, “I need this now”?

### Value proposition

- What changes for the user after using it?
- Is the improvement 10% better or 10x clearer/faster/safer?
- Can the value be demonstrated quickly?

### Differentiation

- Is the idea competing against products, habits, spreadsheets, human services, or doing nothing?
- Is the advantage based on data, workflow, UX, automation, trust, price, network, speed, or domain expertise?
- Can competitors copy it easily?

### UX quality

- What is the first-run experience?
- What are the defaults?
- What does the product do when data is missing?
- What mistakes can users recover from?
- What must be explained, previewed, confirmed, or reversible?

### AI-specific quality

For AI products or AI-assisted features, check:

- Is AI necessary, or would rules/UI/search be better?
- Are outputs verifiable?
- Does the product show uncertainty where needed?
- Can users correct the model?
- Are privacy, consent, and data boundaries clear?
- Are hallucinations, automation errors, and prompt injection considered?
- Does the system improve through feedback without hiding risk?

### Business and execution

- What is the smallest testable version?
- What is the hardest operational dependency?
- What are the cost drivers?
- What does retention depend on?
- What would make this defensible after launch?

## Upgrade path archetypes

Use these when helpful:

### Lean AAA

Cut scope aggressively and make one high-value workflow excellent.

Best when the idea is broad, early, or undervalidated.

### Premium AAA

Optimize polish, trust, onboarding, language, defaults, and reliability.

Best when the target user values quality and confidence.

### AI-native AAA

Use AI to change the workflow, not merely decorate it.

Best when prediction, synthesis, personalization, or automation creates clear leverage.

### Enterprise-grade AAA

Add auditability, permissions, integrations, compliance, admin controls, reporting, and predictable support.

Best when teams or organizations are the buyer.

### Ecosystem AAA

Make the idea plug into tools users already use.

Best when adoption friction is the main risk.
