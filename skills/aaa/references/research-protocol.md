# Research Protocol

Use this when the answer needs current facts, competitive benchmarking, standards, research, platform behavior, library behavior, or industry best practice.

## Research goals

Research should answer:

1. What is the current high-end standard in this domain?
2. What do leading products, teams, standards, or papers suggest?
3. What does the user's idea already satisfy?
4. What is missing or below world-class quality?
5. What upgrade paths are evidence-aligned?

## Source hierarchy

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Official documentation from relevant platforms, frameworks, vendors, or standards bodies
2. Peer-reviewed research, respected preprints, books from recognized experts, or academic labs
3. Public engineering/design/product writing from leading companies
4. Reputable analyst reports or industry surveys
5. High-quality practitioner articles with concrete evidence
6. Forum posts only for field signals, not authoritative claims

## Search strategy

Use multiple query types:

- Current best practices: `[domain] best practices 2026`, `[framework] official guide`, `[standard] guidelines`
- Research: `[domain] research paper`, `[topic] systematic review`, `[topic] empirical study`
- Competitive benchmark: `best [product category] tools`, `[competitor] feature`, `[category] comparison`
- Risk: `[domain] security risks`, `[domain] privacy risks`, `[domain] failure modes`
- Code: `[language/framework] official style guide`, `[framework] architecture best practices`, `[library] documentation version`

Use dates when freshness matters.

## Evidence rules

- Cite every factual claim derived from external sources.
- Distinguish source facts from your interpretation.
- Do not treat a single vendor claim as industry consensus.
- Include uncertainty when sources are weak, old, conflicting, or marketing-heavy.
- For code, prefer exact official docs for the relevant version.
- For regulated or safety-sensitive areas, prefer standards and official guidance.

## Benchmarking method

For product or feature ideas, compare against:

- Best-in-class direct competitors
- Indirect substitutes
- Manual workarounds
- Platform-native defaults
- Emerging AI-native approaches

For code or architecture, compare against:

- Language idioms
- Framework conventions
- Security standards
- Testing conventions
- Operational and maintainability expectations

## Research output discipline

Do not dump every finding. Synthesize into:

- Current high-end standard
- Relevant evidence
- Gaps in the user's idea
- Implications for improvement
- Uncertainties
